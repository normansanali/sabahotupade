Who Owns UPS? UPS Company History

 Posted on August 29, 2024  |  16 minutes  |  3341 words  |  Larita Shotwell

UPS, officially known as United Parcel Service, Inc., is a publicly traded company founded in 1907. The company was originally called the American Messenger Company and specialized in telegraphs. Over the years, UPS has grown to become one of the world’s largest shipping couriers, offering courier and express services, freight forwarding services, and logistics services. It is headquartered in Sandy Springs, Georgia, and operates worldwide. The current CEO of UPS is Carol B. Tomé. As of 2022, UPS had a revenue of US$100.3 billion, operating income of US$13.09 billion, and a net income of US$11.55 billion. It has a total of 536,000 employees and subsidiaries such as UPS Airlines, Nightline Group, Marken, UPS Logistics, UPS Mail Innovations, UPS Professional Solutions, and UPS i-parcel.

History of UPS

Founded on August 28, 1907, in Seattle, Washington, UPS, originally known as the American Messenger Company, has a rich and storied history that spans over a century. Established by James E. Casey and Claude Ryan, the company initially focused on package delivery to retail stores and relied on foot and bicycle deliveries.

In 1913, UPS took a significant step forward by acquiring its first delivery vehicle, a Model T Ford, and merged with a competitor to form Merchants Parcel Delivery. This move marked a significant milestone in the company’s growth, enabling more efficient and expanded delivery capabilities.

As the years passed, UPS continued to evolve and enhance its services. In 1919, the company expanded its operations to Oakland, California, and changed its name to United Parcel Service. This change reflected UPS’s commitment to providing comprehensive package delivery solutions on a larger scale.

One of the key turning points in UPS’s history came in 1929 when the company introduced air service. Recognizing the need for faster and more efficient delivery options, UPS pioneered the use of airplanes for shipping, accelerating the delivery process and expanding its reach across the United States and beyond.

In 1988, UPS reached another milestone by launching UPS Airlines, a dedicated air cargo division that further enhanced the company’s global logistics capabilities. This strategic move solidified UPS’s position as a leader in the shipping and logistics industry.

Throughout its history, UPS has continually embraced innovation, adapting to changing customer needs and technological advancements. From humble beginnings as a local messenger company to becoming a global logistics powerhouse, UPS has remained committed to delivering excellence in package delivery and providing efficient supply chain solutions to customers worldwide.

    UPS Ownership Structure

    UPS is a publicly traded company, allowing ownership to be distributed among various shareholders. Institutional investors, including asset managers and mutual funds, hold a significant percentage of UPS stock ownership. As of the latest data, The Vanguard Group Inc. is one of the largest institutional shareholders, with over 67 million shares and a 9.3% stake in the company. BlackRock Fund Advisors is another major institutional holder, with 7.7% ownership.

    In addition to institutional investors, UPS also has insiders who hold shares in the company. One notable insider shareholder is retired CEO David Abney, who holds the largest insider stake with over 3 million shares.

    UPS Market Share

    UPS, as a key player in the shipping and logistics industry, possesses a significant market share in the United States. In 2021, UPS held an impressive 37% market share of all local deliveries, establishing itself as the largest private parcel delivery company in the country. Close on its heels, FedEx secured a substantial market share of 33%, followed by the United States Postal Service with a share of 17%.
