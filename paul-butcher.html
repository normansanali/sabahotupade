Professional Career

Initially, Paul Butcher began his acting debut at age 7 on a 2001 episode of “The Bernie Mac Show.” Later, he provided a voice to the character Kanta Okagi in the 2001 English dub film “My Neighbor Totoro.”

That same year, he again did a voice role for Philip Albert in “Nico’adventures.” Moreover, he first appeared in an episode of the 2002 drama “Six Feet Under” playing the role of a 5-Year-Old Boy.

Later, he made his guest roles appearances in ‘That “70s Show” (2003), “NYPD Blue” (2004), “Medical Investigation” (2005), and “Bones” (2005). He appeared in two episodes as himself in the sketch comedy television “All That” including the “10th Anniversary Reunion Special” episode.

Moreover, he played the main role of Dustin Brooks in the Nickelodeon comedy-drama television series “Zoey 101” (2005-2008). In the series, he is seen playing the younger brother of the lead character Zoey Brooks, for four seasons. Following this, he made his recurring voice role as Kenny / Caleb / Robin / Arrow Kid #2 in the animated sitcom “King of the Hill” appeared in 5 episodes.

In 2007, he appeared in the role of Alex Rosen in the police procedural drama television series “Without a Trace” and appeared in the episode of One and Only. Later, he played Jeffrey Barton in the 2009 police procedural crime drama series “Criminal Minds.”

In 2013, he did a recurring role of Jeff Pookie in the web transmedia mockumentary sitcom “MyMusic” appearing in 12 episodes. He was cast as Sean Mills in the television movie “A Lesson in Romance” in the year 2014.

After this, he appeared in an episode of the 2015 weekly comedy audio podcast, “Comedy Bang Bang” as Tyler Cunningham. Moreover, he did a recurring role of Brian in the 2016 series “Destiny 7” appearing in 5 episodes.

Musical Career

He worked on three cover versions of the tracks ‘This Love’ by Maroon 5, ‘Gimme That’ by Chris Brown, and ‘Love Yourself’ by Justin Bieber in the year 2008. In the year 2010, he released the non-album single “Like I Would” as well as the promotional singles “Love Yourself,” “Little Things,” “Pillowtalk,” “Chainsaw,” and “Mercy.” After a year, he released the song “Ain’t Hurtin’ Nobody.”

Awards
