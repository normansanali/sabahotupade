Charlie Munger is an American investor and philanthropist who serves as chairman of The Daily Journal Corporation and a director of Costco Wholesale Corporation. He is highly recognized for acting as Warren Buffet’s right-hand man and serving as vice chairman of Berkshire Hathaway Corporation.

When was Charlie Munger born?

On January 1st, 1924, Charlie Munger was born in Omaha, Nebraska. He was born to his parents, Alfred C. and Florence Munger. He went to Omaha for his formal education before moving to Michigan to finish it.

Charlie moved to Michigan and went to the University of Michigan. He decided to major in mathematics there. He dropped out of school and joined the US Army Air Corps, where he eventually attained the rank of Second Lieutenant.

Charlie then decided to complete his studies in meteorology at the California Institute of Technology. He soon graduated with honors from Harvard Law School after enrolling there. As a student, he took part in the Harvard Legal Aid Bureau.

Career

Charlie Munger moved his family to California and started working for the law firm Wright & Garrett. Soon after, he founded the real estate law firm Munger, Tolles & Olson LLP.

After a while, Charlie stopped practicing law and concentrated more on managing the investments and partnering with Otis Booth to develop real estate. The investment firm Wheeler, Munger & Company was quickly created by Charlie and Jack Wheeler. But after experiencing large losses, Charlie and his business partner decided to shut down.

Wesco Financial Corporation was led by Charlie Munger from 2004 to 2011, and it is now wholly owned by Berkshire Hathaway. The Berkshire Hathaway Corporation oversees a number of enterprises and holds stock in companies including Coca-Cola, Wells Fargo, Kraft Foods, etc.

Charlie is also a director of Costco Wholesale Corporation and chairman of the Daily Journal Corporation. He acquired enough shares of Berkshire Hathaway Corporation to become Warren Buffet’s partner.
