The Meaning Behind The Song: Back in the 90s by GROUPLOVE

My Personal Connection

As a Music Therapist, I often come across songs that have profound meanings and leave lasting impressions on me. “Back in the 90’s” by GROUPLOVE is one such song that struck a chord with me in a unique way. I first heard this song on a rainy afternoon, and I remember stumbling upon it at a friend’s house. From the very first verse, I was captivated by its nostalgic yet introspective lyrics.

The Song’s Context

“Back in the 90’s” is the ending credits song for the Netflix series “BoJack Horseman.” This animated show, known for its dark humor and exploration of mental health issues, revolves around the life of a washed-up sitcom star, BoJack Horseman. The song serves as a reflection on BoJack’s past and his struggles with understanding his identity.

An Exploration of Identity

In the song, we hear the lyrics, “And I’m trying to hold onto my past, It’s been so long, I don’t think I’m gonna last, I guess, I’m just trying to make you understand, That I’m more horse than a man, Or I’m more man than a horse.” These lines depict BoJack’s constant battle with his own identity. As a horse-man hybrid, he grapples with whether he identifies more as a horse or as a man. This struggle mirrors our own human need to understand and define ourselves.

Nostalgia and Longing

The chorus of the song, with its catchy melody, goes, “Back in the ’90s, I was in a very famous TV show, I’m BoJack the Horseman, BoJack the Horseman, don’t act like you don’t know.” Through these lines, BoJack longs for the past when he was the star of a successful TV show. He yearns for the recognition and validation he once had, reflecting a common human experience of nostalgia and longing for times gone by.

A Wider Meaning

While “Back in the 90’s” is written specifically for the character BoJack Horseman, the song resonates on a broader level. It speaks to the universal struggle of finding one’s identity and the longing for the past. We all have moments where we yearn for a time when things were simpler or when we felt more secure in our sense of self.

The Power of Music

GROUPLOVE’s “Back in the 90’s” is a powerful song that not only complements the themes of the TV show but also stands on its own as a representation of the human experience. As a music therapist, I often turn to music to explore and express complex emotions. This song reminds me of the therapeutic potential of music, as it allows us to delve deep into our own identities, memories, and emotions.

In conclusion, “Back in the 90’s” by GROUPLOVE holds deep meanings within its lyrics and melody. It serves as a mirror for our own struggles with identity and the powerful emotions associated with nostalgia. Music, such as this song, has the ability to connect with us on a profound level and provide a platform for self-reflection and understanding.
