Adam Driver Biography

Adam Douglas Driver commonly referred to as Adam Driver, is an American actor famous for his breakout role on the TV series ‘Girls,’. He is also famous for his big-screen work such as ‘Inside Llewyn Davis’ and the ‘Star Wars’ franchise.

Adam was born in San Diego, California but his family moved to Indiana when he was 7 years old. He went to Mishawaka High School. After graduating from high school he enrolled at the University of Indianapolis for a year. He then chose to join an acting school at New York’s popular Juilliard.

Adam Driver Movies

Adam made his feature film debut in the biographical film by Clint Eastwood J. Edgar that was released in 2011. The show was quite a success that he bagged three nominations for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series for the character he played.

In that same year, he featured in minor roles in two big films, as a cipher officer Samuel Beckwith in Steven Spielberg’s historical drama Lincoln, and Lev Shapiro in Noah Baumbach’s comedy-drama Frances Ha.

Adam Driver Marine

The terrorist attacks of September 11, 2001, convinced Adam to join the military. He subsequently enlisted in the U.S. Marines and served for more than two years.

His army dreams were shattered when he got a sternum injury due to a mountain-biking accident. Due to his injury, he was honorably discharged.

Adam has also featured in the drama Not Waving But Drowning and the romantic-comedy Gayby. He received major off-Broadway acceptance and recognition for his role as Cliff, a working-class Welsh houseguest in Look Back in Anger. The film did well as he won the Lucille Lortel Award for Outstanding Featured Actor in a Play.
